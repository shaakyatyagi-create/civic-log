How We Sustain Ourselves

MARKET VIABILITY

Civic Log stays free for citizens. Here's how the platform is designed to sustain itself financially without ever charging the public a subscription.

01

Municipal Dashboard Licensing

Municipal corporations and state civic bodies license the analytics dashboard — hotspot maps, solve-rate leaderboards, resolution-time benchmarks — as a paid oversight tool for their own departments.

02

CSR Sponsorships

Corporates fund a district or category cleanup drive through their CSR budget and get visible, provable impact tied to real before/after civic data — stronger ROI than a plaque.

03

Contractor Lead Generation

Local plumbers, electricians, and waste-management companies pay per qualified lead matched to the relevant complaint category — a marketplace built on real, timestamped demand.

04

Real-Estate & Insurance Data Licensing

Anonymized, aggregated locality-level civic health data is licensed to real-estate portals and insurers for neighborhood scoring and risk modeling.

05

Direct-Sold Local Advertising

Local businesses and NGOs buy placement directly on district and category pages — higher-yield than generic ad networks, and relevant to the citizens actually viewing them.
